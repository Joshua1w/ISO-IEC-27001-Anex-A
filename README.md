ISO/IEC 27001 Anex A: Control Implementation 

Standard: ISO/IEC 27001:2022 
Control's location: Annex A 
Total number of controls: 93 controls 

ISO/IEC 27001 Overview 
ISO/IEC 27001 is an international standard for establishing and managing an Information Security Management System (ISMS) using a risk-based approach to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. 
Annex A of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 defines 93 security controls across four areas: organizational, people, physical, and technological, covering governance, human risk, physical protection, and technical security. 
Controls are selected based on risk and documented in a Statement of Applicability (SoA), ensuring security measures are proportionate, auditable, and aligned with business and regulatory requirements. 
ISO/IEC 27001 Control Structure 
ISO/IEC 27001:2022 defines its information security controls in Annex A, organized into four themes that address security across the organization. 
Organizational controls focus on governance, policies, risk ownership, supplier security, incident management, and regulatory compliance. 
People's controls address human-related risks through screening, training, defined responsibilities, and secure onboarding and offboarding. 
Physical controls protect facilities, equipment, and physical environments from unauthorized access, damage, or disruption. 
Technological controls provide technical safeguards for systems, networks, applications, and data, including access control, monitoring, secure development, and data protection. 
Together, these control groups ensure information security is managed holistically using a risk-based and auditable approach aligned with business objectives.
